Methodology

We appreciate that there is no such thing as a single methodology for all data science problems. But we have been there many times, and learned how to take pragmatic decisions to progress steadily – and sometimes cut losses promptly.

  1. Understand your business, products, data, technology, and architecture. The first phase of building any data science solution is to understand the product aspiration, its business objectives and the type of data available to achieve them. This conceptual understanding allows us to create a high level data science design. Read more...—
    We then need to sample just enough representative data to configure a prototype Gixante, so that it can start looking for interesting patterns and help us assess the potential of the project. The initial prototype will be built using streamlined modules, but it will be assembled as a complete pipeline. Thanks to Gixante’s fully modular design, modules can then be swapped iteratively to choose the best configuration. At this stage we aim to have several iterations per day, keeping a detailed log of the parameters and results of these experiments. In order to exploit its modularity, the data needs to be standardised: although the algorithms and programs used for pre and post processing are not in their final form, it is crucial to understand whether they can be fully scaled up in production systems later on.
  2. Create business benchmarks and data product roadmap. The prototype must serve two main purposes: give a realistic assessment of what business objectives can be achieved and spell out the corresponding engineering requirements. Correspondingly, during the prototyping stage we will work closely with you to define a set of business metrics to benchmark Gixante’s performance and eventually an integrated data product roadmap. Read more...—
    These benchmarks will allow for scientific hypothesis validation and product development, as well as giving full transparency on our process. The roadmap will define priorities and resource allocation so that they can be aligned with the other teams involved.
  3. Assemble Gixante’s modules and deploy. Once Gixante has achieved the performance and maturity agreed in the roadmap, it is time to embed it into any existing architecture, whether local or cloud based. This usually consists in translating the pre and post processing steps followed in the prototyping stage into more mature technology to ensure automation, stability and robustness. Read more...—
    Although we use Deep Learning to create data features in an unsupervised fashion, it is likely that some components built to standardise the data will have custom elements. The stage is set for Gixante to be deployed in production. The custom data will be standardised before being modelled, flow to Gixante and return enriched in a modular and streamlined pipeline.
  4. Maintenance and continuous development. We will continuously collect and report on performance and quality through our API, so that Gixante’s future developments are clearly benchmarked for the specific product. Gixante’s modules are indeed continuously updated to exploit cutting edge advancements in Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence and High Performance Computing, but they will always maintain the same input and output requirements for backward compatibility. Read more...—
    Only after they have been thoroughly tested, they will be deployed in your embedded pipeline with no development required at your end. Should your data features or product requirements change, we will update the embedded pre and post processing modules respectively.
